Aluminum Profile Extrusion Technical FAQ

This page targets FAQ-style search intent around custom aluminum extrusion profiles, alloy and temper direction, powder coated or anodized sections, tooling planning, and drawing-based export RFQ preparation.

Direct answer

Profile extrusion sourcing becomes clearer when buyers define section drawing, application, alloy and temper direction, finish, quantity, and processing scope before asking for a final quotation. Short “extrusion price per kg” requests do not capture the full commercial picture.

FAQ highlights

  • What should buyers send first for a custom aluminum profile RFQ? Buyers should send section drawing, application, alloy or temper direction if known, finish requirement, length, quantity, and packing expectation.
  • Why do alloy, temper, and finish need to be discussed together? Because extrusion feasibility, mechanical direction, surface treatment, and final commercial fit are linked. Quoting finish without application context can lead to the wrong recommendation.
  • When do tooling and secondary processing become important? They become important when buyers need a new section, tighter processing control, drilling, cutting, slotting, or a more complete component supply route instead of only raw extrusion.
