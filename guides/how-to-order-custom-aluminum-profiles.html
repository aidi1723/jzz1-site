How to Order Custom Aluminum Profiles

Custom extrusion orders move faster when buyers send the right technical inputs first. This guide covers the minimum information needed to discuss tooling, finish, quantity, and export packing without wasting quotation cycles.

Section drawingTooling checkFinish options

Core inputs for quotation

  • Section drawing or sample photo
  • Alloy and temper if known
  • Finish requirement
  • Length and quantity
  • Packing or bundle direction
  • Destination country or port

Tooling discussion

For new sections, tooling is often part of the first conversation. Buyers should clarify whether the profile is a one-off project shape or a repeat-use section for long-term orders.

Finish discussion

Powder coating, anodizing, and wood-grain transfer all change lead time and production logic. If color matters, it should be defined early.

Recommended order flow

  1. Send section drawing and application.
  2. Confirm tooling feasibility and finish direction.
  3. Align quantity, cutting length, and packing.
  4. Approve samples or details before mass production.
