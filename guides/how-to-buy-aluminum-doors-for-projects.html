How to Buy Aluminum Doors for Projects

This guide is for contractors, distributors, and project buyers who need aluminum doors quoted and produced with fewer back-and-forth revisions. The goal is simple: confirm the right inputs before production starts.

Project schedulesHardware confirmationPacking labels

What to confirm first

  • Door type: entrance, exterior, single, or double door
  • Size schedule or drawing set
  • Opening direction and hardware direction
  • Glass, panel, threshold, and finish requirements
  • Destination market and packing method

Why buyers lose time

  • Only rough dimensions are provided
  • Hardware brands or functions are not defined
  • Packing labels are discussed too late
  • Finish samples are not aligned before production

What a good RFQ looks like

  • PDF or CAD door schedule
  • Quantity by model
  • Glass or solid panel direction
  • Hardware preference
  • Destination port and target lead time

Recommended buying flow

  1. Send door schedule or drawings.
  2. Confirm model breakdown, hardware, and finish.
  3. Agree on project packing and labels.
  4. Approve production, inspection, and shipment plan.
