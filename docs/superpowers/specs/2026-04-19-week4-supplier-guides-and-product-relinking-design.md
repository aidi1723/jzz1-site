# Week 4 Supplier Guides And Product Relinking Design

## Background

Week 1 to Week 3 already built the first-tier market structure for Singapore, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The site now has market pages, product hubs, Guide pages, and Knowledge Base pages that can support more specific project queries.

The next bottleneck is not generic topic coverage. It is the lack of supplier-intent pages that sit between broad market discovery and a real RFQ. Week 4 focuses on that missing layer and then pushes those pages back into the core product hubs.

## Goal

- Add 3 supplier-intent Guide pages for the first-tier markets
- Strengthen `products/windows.html`, `products/doors.html`, and `products/sliding-aluminum-windows.html` with clearer supplier-intent routes
- Update market pages, Guides hub, sitemap, and SEO execution docs so the new URLs are part of the crawlable structure

## Week 4 Pages

### Singapore

- `guides/singapore-condo-window-supplier-rfq-guide.html`
- Target keywords:
  - `singapore condo windows supplier`
  - `singapore aluminum windows supplier`
  - `singapore acoustic window supplier`

Role:

- Capture supplier-comparison and RFQ-preparation intent for condo and high-rise window buyers
- Bridge Singapore market traffic into the aluminum windows product hub and existing acoustic/spec pages

### Malaysia

- `guides/malaysia-hotel-window-and-door-supplier-guide.html`
- Target keywords:
  - `malaysia hotel window and door supplier`
  - `malaysia balcony door supplier`
  - `malaysia aluminum windows supplier`

Role:

- Capture supplier-intent around hotel and hospitality packages
- Add a more direct supplier route beyond the existing commercial RFQ page and balcony-door scenario page

### Indonesia

- `guides/indonesia-commercial-window-supplier-project-guide.html`
- Target keywords:
  - `indonesia commercial window supplier`
  - `indonesia aluminum windows supplier`
  - `indonesia coastal project windows`

Role:

- Capture commercial-project and supplier-qualification intent
- Connect Indonesia market traffic with windows, mixed-package RFQ, and facade-related project routes

## Content Structure

All three new pages should follow the existing Guide pattern:

- Hero with direct search-intent answer
- Buyer-type or project-fit split
- Supplier qualification or RFQ comparison table
- Friction section showing why quotation rounds slow down
- Cleaner RFQ or supplier checklist section
- Related pages section for cluster relinking
