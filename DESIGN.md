# DESIGN.md

## Design Intent

- Product archetype: International B2B manufacturer and export supplier for aluminum and uPVC building systems.
- Primary use cases: Supplier discovery, quotation conversion, technical trust-building, market-specific entry pages, and long-tail procurement research.
- Overall mood: Architectural, engineered, export-ready, calm, premium, credible.
- Keywords: Precision, specification-first, material confidence, international trade, factory proof, modern construction.
- Non-goals: Startup gradient style, AI landing-page theatrics, generic China supplier look, crowded catalog-page visuals, loud discount-commerce energy.
- Best-fit screens: Home, product hubs, market pages, solutions hub, technical content hubs, contact and RFQ pages.

## Color System

- Background: `#f5f2ec` warm mineral white
- Background alt: `#ebe6de` soft stone
- Surface: `#ffffff`
- Surface elevated: `#fbf8f2`
- Primary text: `#151515`
- Secondary text: `#5f615d`
- Accent: `#b88d3b` champagne gold
- Accent contrast: `#111111`
- Border: `#d8d1c4`
- Success: `#2f6952`
- Warning: `#a67118`
- Danger: `#a44d3f`
- Neutral dark / shell: `#1c1d20` graphite
- Neutral steel: `#8d9296`

Use accent sparingly. Gold should signal quality, key highlights, and active emphasis, not flood large areas. Large surfaces should stay quiet, mineral, and architectural.

## Typography

- Sans family: Manrope, Geist Sans, or another clean neo-grotesk with precise proportions
- Mono family: IBM Plex Mono or Geist Mono for specs, labels, dimensions, and quoting cues
- Display style: Large, tight, calm, confident
- Body style: Neutral, readable, B2B-direct, medium measure
- Tracking and casing rules: Sentence case for most UI; uppercase only for compact labels and technical markers

The type should feel more like a modern architecture studio or premium industrial supplier than a generic SaaS startup.

## Layout and Spacing

- Max content width: `1240px`
- Preferred density: Moderate; enough air to feel premium without becoming empty
- Mobile behavior: Collapse into a clean single-column story with preserved hierarchy and persistent CTA access

The homepage should not feel like a sitemap. It should read as a guided narrative: value proposition, product architecture, proof, process, and conversion.

## Shape and Surface

- Corner radius: `18px` to `30px` depending on section importance
- Border style: 1px warm-gray borders, always visible but subtle
- Shadow style: Soft and shallow, low blur, low opacity
- Texture, grain, or gradient usage: Very subtle warm gradients or metal-inspired fades are acceptable; avoid colorful gradients

The site should feel like brushed aluminum, coated metal, export packaging, and architectural detailing.

## Imagery Direction

- Favor real factory, packing, detail, and installed-system imagery over stock photos
- Prefer imagery without visible people, presenters, or posed sales-style shots
- Avoid short-video cover frames with hosts, talking-head compositions, or presenter-centered shots
- Avoid images with baked-in text overlays and buttons
- Show material quality, profile detail, export readiness, and project relevance
- Pair wide context shots with close-up detail shots when possible

Imagery should prove capability and quality, not just fill space.

## Navigation

- Reduce the number of visible top-level links
- Group country pages under market positioning on the homepage rather than exposing everything in the header
- Keep a persistent CTA such as `Request Quote`

## Homepage Structure

1. Compact premium header
2. Hero with clean image, strong claim, proof chips, and CTA
3. Trust strip with metrics or capability markers
4. Product architecture section
5. Market or buyer-type section
6. Technical solutions and standards section
7. Factory, quality, and export evidence section
8. Selected project references and buying pages
9. Short buying process
10. Final RFQ CTA

## Do / Do Not

- Do: Make the site feel like a premium architectural supplier
- Do: Use real imagery as trust evidence
- Do: Separate proof-heavy sections from generic content cards
- Do: Keep the experience international, clean, and spec-friendly
- Do not: Keep the old long flat navigation
- Do not: Use hero images with embedded text or people
- Do not: Make every section the same card grid
- Do not: Overuse gold, gradients, shadows, or animation
